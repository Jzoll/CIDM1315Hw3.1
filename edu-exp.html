Education and Experience

During my academic career, I've had the opportunity to earn four Associate's degrees. I studied Liberal Arts and Natural and Physical Sciences in high school and Architecture and Diagnostic Medical Sonography in college. I've had a longer journey than most in search of my passion and I am grateful to have had so much exposure to the different paths I might take. Most recently, I've begun to pursue a degree in Computer Information Systems. It's been less than a semester so far, but I feel a passion for the broad creative and industrial possibilities of my chosen field.

Despite my as yet lacking experience in professional engineering, I've been lucky to have a broad scope of personal experiences that I believe will help me perform well in any setting. I've had the opportunity to travel all over the country and even visit our neighbors to the north and south. This exposure to different places, people, and cultures has been vital to my understanding of the world and of myself, and it has allowed me to collaborate effectively with all kinds of people. As a part of this exposure, I was also able to reconnect with my Mexican heritage and learn Spanish. Particularly in Texas, this knowledge of Spanish and Mexican norms will give me an edge when communicating with a diverse team of coworkers.

I've also been fortunate enough to pursue many creative endeavors. Each of these pursuits has taught me something different. Ceramics taught me the importance of perseverance in the face of failure. Dance taught me to express myself boldly. Yoga taught me to look inward and to be patient. Each of these skills will translate well to any professional environment. I believe that these experiences and skills have made me a more well-rounded person who will be better able to follow through on her goals and collaborate with anybody regardless of the challenges we face.
